KeepSafe – Fintech Mobile Application

To develop a mobile application that empowers users to securely manage their finances without reliance on physical banking branches. KeepSafe focuses on delivering an intuitive user experience, robust security, and comprehensive financial management tools, including multi-currency support, card management, and quick transactions.

Value Proposition

KeepSafe stands out by offering:
• Enhanced Security: Advanced biometric login, multi-factor authentication, and real-time alerts for suspicious activity.
• Comprehensive Financial Management: Manage both virtual and physical cards, switch between currencies, and execute fast, secure transactions.
• User-Centric Design: Simple navigation, customizable UI, and accessibility features to cater to diverse user needs.

Key Features

• Onboarding & Authentication: Quick registration with biometric login options for enhanced security.
• Multi-Currency Support: Manage local and foreign accounts with a seamless switching experience.
• Card Management: Block, view, and manage both physical and virtual cards securely.
• Transfers & Payments: Fast, reliable money transfers, bill payments, and airtime purchases.
• Security Features: Biometric login, transaction PINs, and real-time fraud detection.

Target Audience

• Young Professionals: Frequent digital payments, personal and business expense management.
• Frequent Travelers: Need for multi-currency card management and global transactions.
• Small Business Owners: High transaction volume, requiring streamlined financial tracking.

Challenges and Constraints

• Security vs Usability: Balancing robust security measures with a frictionless user experience.
• Currency Management: Simplifying multi-currency handling while maintaining accuracy and speed.
• Performance Optimization: Ensuring the app remains responsive under high transaction loads.

Market Context

The Nigerian fintech market is rapidly expanding, driven by a young, mobile-first population with a growing demand for digital financial services. However, traditional banking apps often fail to address the unique needs of these users, creating significant market gaps and pain points.

User Pain Points

From user interviews and surveys, we identified three critical pain points that the KeepSafe app aims to solve:

• Terrible User Interfaces – Users often struggle with outdated, clunky designs that require too many steps for simple transactions.
Data Insight: 65% of 500 surveyed mobile banking users in 2023 cited poor design and confusing navigation as their primary frustration.

• Limited Features and Flexibility – Many banking apps lack the ability to store multiple cards, manage budgets, or automate bill payments.
Data Insight: 70% of users in a 2024 feedback survey expressed a desire for comprehensive wallet features, including multiple card storage and expense tracking.

• Physical Bank Dependency – During the COVID-19 pandemic, the inability to access physical bank branches left many users without essential financial services.
Data Insight: 85% of users reported that they had to visit physical branches or use alternative financial apps due to the limitations of their existing banking apps.

Product Requirements Document (PRD)

The Product Requirements Document (PRD) outlines the core functionality, user stories, and acceptance criteria for the KeepSafe mobile app. This document serves as a blueprint for the 3-month development timeline, focusing on delivering a secure, user-friendly, and feature-rich fintech solution.

Project Goals

• Security: Protect user data with biometric login, transaction PINs, and multi-factor authentication.
• Convenience: Simplify financial management with features like saved cards, multi-currency support, and quick transfers.
• User Retention: Provide a seamless, low-friction experience to drive early adoption and long-term loyalty.

Core User Stories and Acceptance Criteria

• Secure Onboarding – As a user, I want to register quickly and securely so I can start managing my finances without hassle.
Acceptance Criteria: Users can complete onboarding in under 2 minutes, including biometric setup.

• Biometric Login – As a user, I want to log in using biometrics for quick, secure access.
Acceptance Criteria: Biometric login works on 95% of attempts, with fallback to PIN or password.

• Multi-Currency Management – As a frequent traveler, I want to switch between currencies easily so I can manage my international expenses.
Acceptance Criteria: Users can switch between currency wallets in under 5 seconds.

• Card Management – As a business owner, I want to store, block, and manage multiple cards securely.
Acceptance Criteria: Users can add, block, and view card details without errors.

• Fast Transfers – As a student, I want to send money quickly to friends and family without repeated data entry.
Acceptance Criteria: Transfers are processed within 10 seconds, with instant notifications.

• Real-Time Alerts – As a user, I want to receive real-time notifications for all transactions to stay in control of my finances.
Acceptance Criteria: 95% of alerts are delivered within 2 seconds of transaction completion.

Success Metrics

• User Retention: 60% of users returning within 2 weeks – Indicates early product-market fit.
• Transaction Speed: 98% of transactions completed in < 5 seconds – Ensures fast, frictionless experience.
• Biometric Login Success Rate: 95% – Critical for security and convenience.
• User Satisfaction: 90% positive feedback on app speed and usability – Key for long-term growth and referrals.
• Feature Adoption: 50% of users engaging with card management features within 3 months – Validates core use case.

Technical Requirements

• Authentication: Biometric login (Face ID, Touch ID), multi-factor authentication (2FA).
• Security: End-to-end encryption, transaction PINs, real-time fraud detection.
• Data Storage: Secure storage for card details, encrypted transaction history.
• Notifications: Real-time alerts for transactions, low balance, and suspicious activity.
• API Integrations: Secure APIs for multi-currency conversion, card verification, and payment processing.
• Analytics: Built-in tracking for user behavior, feature engagement, and retention metrics.

Constraints and Assumptions

• Security First: The app must prioritize security without adding unnecessary friction.
• Scalability: Designed to handle high transaction volumes without compromising performance.
• Cross-Platform Consistency: Consistent experience across iOS and Android.
• Offline Mode: Basic functionality should be available even with limited internet connectivity.

Product Design & Prototyping

The design process for KeepSafe focused on creating a secure, intuitive, and visually appealing experience that aligns with the app’s core values of speed, security, and convenience. The design system was built to be scalable and consistent across both iOS and Android, ensuring a seamless experience for all users.

Design Principles

• Security First: Clear visual cues for secure actions like biometric login and card blocking.
• User-Centric Simplicity: Minimalist UI with a focus on clarity, ease of use, and reduced cognitive load.
• High Accessibility: Clear typography, strong color contrasts, and intuitive iconography for all users.
• Speed and Responsiveness: Fast loading times, optimized animations, and clear feedback on user actions.

Wireframes and Low-Fidelity Designs

• Login and Onboarding: Simple, secure biometric setup with clear progress indicators.
• Dashboard: Clean overview of accounts, balances, and recent transactions for quick access.
• Card Management: Centralized card dashboard with quick actions like lock, block, and view details.
• Transaction Flow: Streamlined, one-tap transfers with saved beneficiaries and card shortcuts.

High-Fidelity Prototypes

• Color Palette: Predominantly red and white for a sense of security and urgency, with light/dark mode options for personalization.
• Typography: Clean, modern typeface for readability and speed.
• Iconography: Simple, intuitive icons to reduce cognitive load and guide users through complex actions.
• Micro-Interactions: Subtle animations for actions like biometric login, card management, and transaction confirmations to enhance user feedback.

Design System and Branding

• Primary Colors: Red (#E53935), White (#FFFFFF), and Dark Grey (#212121) for a professional yet approachable feel.
• Typography: Clear, sans-serif fonts for readability on small screens.
• Iconography: Custom, easily recognizable icons for key actions like “Send Money,” “Add Card,” and “View Transactions.”
• Tone of Voice: Confident, professional, and supportive, reflecting the app’s commitment to security and user empowerment.

Key Design Decisions

• Onboarding Flow: Simplified to reduce drop-off rates, with biometric setup integrated into the first-time login process.
• Card Management UI: Large, tappable cards with clear action buttons for blocking, unblocking, and viewing details.
• Real-Time Alerts: Clearly differentiated alerts for critical actions like low balance, suspicious transactions, and card activity.
• Personalization: Light and dark mode options for different usage contexts and user preferences.

Accessibility Considerations

• High Contrast Mode: For visually impaired users.
• VoiceOver Compatibility: Designed for seamless integration with iOS and Android screen readers.
• Large Tap Targets: To reduce errors and improve usability on small screens.
• Keyboard Navigation: Optimized for users who rely on external keyboards.

Design Iterations Based on User Testing

• Biometric Authentication: Reduced the number of steps for first-time setup based on early user feedback.
• Card Management Flow: Simplified menu structure for easier navigation, including a dedicated “Manage Cards” tab.
• Transaction Confirmation: Redesigned confirmation screens to clearly display transaction details, reducing user errors.

Agile Execution and Sprint Planning

To keep the KeepSafe project within the 3-month timeline, a scrum-based approach was chosen, focusing on rapid iterations, clear priorities, and tight feedback loops. The project was divided into 3 major sprints, each designed to build and validate critical features quickly.

Sprint Goals and Structure

• Sprint 1 – Core Functionality: Build secure user authentication and fast onboarding.
• Sprint 2 – Financial Features: Implement multi-currency support, card management, and quick transfers.
• Sprint 3 – Security and Personalization: Add real-time alerts, spending limits, and dark mode for personalization.

Sprint 1: Core Functionality

• Biometric login (95% success rate in tests).
• PIN setup and management.
• User account creation and verification.
• Basic dashboard setup with balance overview.
• Initial user testing for login flow.

Sprint 2: Financial Features

• Multi-currency wallet setup.
• Card management (add, block, view details).
• Fast, reliable money transfers.
• Saved beneficiaries for repeat payments.
• Basic spending insights.

Sprint 3: Security and Personalization

• Real-time alerts for transactions and suspicious activity.
• Spending limits and budgeting tools.
• Light/dark mode for personalized user experience.
• In-app notifications for critical updates.
• Final user testing and refinements.

Backlog Management and Prioritization

Features were prioritized based on user feedback, competitive analysis, and technical feasibility. The MoSCoW method was used to ensure that critical features were built first, while lower-priority items were deferred to future releases.

Standup Cadence and Retrospectives

• Daily Standups: 15-minute sessions to discuss progress, blockers, and next steps.
• Sprint Reviews: End-of-sprint demos to validate progress and gather early feedback.
• Retrospectives: Post-sprint reflections to identify what worked, what didn’t, and how to improve.

Challenges and Workarounds

• Security vs Speed: Balancing fast logins with robust security led to the adoption of biometric authentication as the default.
• Complex Card Management: Early user tests revealed the need for clearer card actions, leading to a redesigned card management dashboard.
• Multi-Currency Complexity: Streamlined currency switching based on user feedback to reduce cognitive load.

Key Learnings and Retrospective

• Balancing Security and Usability: Biometric login significantly improved user trust without adding friction.
• Simplified Card Management: Early user feedback drove key UI improvements, reducing confusion and task completion times.
• Continuous User Feedback is Critical: Regular testing cycles helped identify pain points early, reducing the risk of post-launch churn.
